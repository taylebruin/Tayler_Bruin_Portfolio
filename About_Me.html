About Me

As a senior at Brigham Young University with an expected graduation date of June 2023, I have gained a diverse range of experiences throughout my academic and professional career. While I initially pursued a degree in Computer Science, I later transitioned to Journalism, although I continued to cultivate my passion for technology through coursework in the IT department. One particular course that stands out is IT210 Web Development, where I participated in a bootcamp-style program that provided hands-on experience with various technologies to create CRUD functionalities for a task list, as well as comprehensive knowledge of web structure, unit testing, and other key concepts.

My time spent in the field of Journalism has also proven to be a valuable asset in my current role at the International Center for Law and Religious Studies. Here, I have had the opportunity to apply both my technical and communication skills to develop websites that are not only effective and functional but also visually appealing. In addition, I have had the privilege of training others in various settings, such as my current role, where I am responsible for training my replacement by guiding him through accessing our GitHub and setting up development environments, among other tools.

Prior to my current position, I worked in live graphics for several years, both at BYU Broadcasting and as a freelancer for organizations such as the Pac-12. In those roles, I developed a talent for training and guiding others to accomplish complex technical tasks in high-pressure, fast-paced environments. Additionally, during my mission for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, I was responsible for training other missionaries in various roles, including as a "Trainer" for new missionaries as their first companion and as a "District Leader" responsible for overseeing the performance of eight other missionaries.

Overall, my unique combination of technical expertise and communication skills make me a valuable addition to any team.
